📄 Document Preparation Guide

1. FBI Background Check (Channeler Method ONLY)

⏰ TIME-CRITICAL: Start this on Day 1 of your visa process

Goal: FBI background check in 24-72 hours

The Only Viable Method: FBI-Approved Channeler

FBI-approved channelers provide digital fingerprinting (Live Scan) and submit electronically to FBI. Results delivered in 24-72 hours.

Accurate Biometrics (RECOMMENDED)

  • 825+ locations nationwide
  • Website: accuratebiometrics.com
  • Cost: $40-100
  • Results: 24-72 hours

Other Options

  • Fieldprint
  • Idemia
  • Check channeler websites for locations

Process:

  1. Visit channeler website and find nearest location
  2. Book appointment online
  3. Arrive with photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  4. Get fingerprints taken (Live Scan, 5 minutes)
  5. Pay $40-100
  6. Tell them: "For international employment in Korea, E-2 visa"
  7. Receive PDF by email in 24-72 hours

❌ NEVER:

  • DO NOT apply directly to FBI (14-16 weeks, impractical)
  • DO NOT use state/local police checks (not accepted for E-2)
  • DO NOT try to rush FBI direct process (impossible)

2. Bachelor's Degree – Notarization for State Apostille

✅ MUST DO: Name on degree must match passport exactly (including middle names)

Process:

Step 1: Prepare Your Degree

  • Locate your Bachelor's degree diploma (4-year degree required)
  • Make a high-quality photocopy
  • If degree is in Latin: Get English version from university first

Step 2: Get Notarized

  • Find a notary public:
    • Your bank (often free for customers)
    • UPS Store
    • Shipping stores (FedEx Office, etc.)
    • Mobile notaries (search "notary near me")
  • Bring: Original diploma + photocopy + photo ID
  • Request: "Notarized true copy" certification
  • Cost: $10-30
  • Time: 10 minutes

Common Issues to Avoid:

  • ❌ Middle name missing from degree but on passport → Contact university for corrected diploma
  • ❌ Name changed after marriage → Use maiden name degree + marriage certificate (apostilled)
  • ❌ Degree in Latin → Get English version or official translation first
  • ❌ Associate's degree (2-year) → Not acceptable; must be Bachelor's (4-year)

3. Monument Visa - Professional Apostille Service

Why Monument Visa?

  • Federal apostille (FBI): 10 days vs 4-6 weeks DIY
  • State apostille (degree): 2-3 days vs 2-4 weeks DIY
  • Handles both correctly (FBI→Federal, Degree→State)
  • Mixing these up = automatic rejection

Cost: $200-300 total for both apostilles

Website: monumentvisa.com

4. Passport Photos

Specifications:

  • Size: 2×2 inches (U.S. standard) or 3.5×4.5cm (Korea standard)
  • Background: White
  • Recent: Taken within 6 months
  • Requirements: No glasses, no hat, facing forward
  • Color photos

Where to Get: CVS Pharmacy, Walgreens, UPS Store, Costco

How Many: Get 6-8 total copies

Cost: $10-20 for 6-8 photos

5. Additional Documents

  • Passport copy: Color photocopy of bio page
  • Transcripts: Official sealed transcripts from university
  • Self-Health Statement: Download from Korean consulate website
  • Resume/CV: Signed and dated
  • Employment contract: Original signed by you and employer

📦 Packing & Shipping to Korea

Document Order (Top to Bottom)

  1. ✅ FBI Identity History Summary with Federal apostille stapled
  2. ✅ Bachelor's degree copy with State apostille stapled
  3. ✅ Self-Health Statement (signed and dated)
  4. ✅ Passport bio page copy (color, high-res)
  5. ✅ Signed employment contract (original)
  6. ✅ Resume/CV (signed and dated)
  7. ✅ 2-4 passport photos (in small envelope)
  8. ✅ University transcripts (sealed)

✅ DO:

  • Use rigid cardboard mailer (documents lay flat)
  • Place photos in protective sleeve
  • Include cover memo with your contact info
  • Seal securely with packing tape
  • Double-check all documents before sealing

❌ DON'T:

  • Don't use regular envelope (documents will bend)
  • Don't fold any documents
  • Don't glue photos directly
  • Don't remove apostille staples
  • Don't send your original passport to Korea

Shipping Requirements

✅ Use ONLY These Couriers:

  • FedEx International Priority
  • DHL Express Worldwide
  • UPS Worldwide Expedited

Service Level:

  • 2-5 business day delivery
  • Tracking included
  • Signature required on delivery

Cost: $40-80

❌ NEVER Use:

  • Any service without full tracking

Tracking Protocol

❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1. How long does E-2 visa application take?

A. Using the fast method (FBI Channeler + Monument Visa): 5-7 weeks

Using the regular method (direct FBI application): 16-20 weeks, which risks missing your hiring window.

Q2. What is the total cost?

A. $320-525 total.

This is essential investment to protect your $24,000+ annual job.

Q3. What is an FBI Channeler? Is it required?

A. FBI Channeler is an FBI-approved company that submits fingerprints digitally and receives results in 24-72 hours.

Direct FBI application takes 14-16 weeks, so Channeler is essential.

Q4. What is Monument Visa and why do I need it?

A. Monument Visa is a professional apostille expediting service.

  • FBI apostille: 10 days (vs 4-6 weeks regular)
  • Degree apostille: 2-3 days (vs 2-4 weeks regular)

Fast processing is essential, so Monument Visa is highly recommended.

Q5. What is an Apostille?

A. An apostille is authentication that makes U.S. documents valid for use abroad.

  • FBI Background Check: Requires Federal apostille (U.S. Department of State)
  • Degree Copy: Requires State apostille (Secretary of State of the issuing state)

⚠️ Mixing these up = automatic rejection!

Q6. What is a VIN?

A. VIN (Visa Issuance Number) is a confirmation number issued by Korean Immigration.

Your employer submits documents to immigration, receives VIN in 7-10 business days, then you use it to apply for your visa at the consulate.

Q7. Which Korean consulate should I go to?

A. You must apply at the consulate in your jurisdiction (based on residence).

  • Washington DC: East Coast
  • LA, San Francisco: West Coast
  • Chicago: Midwest
  • New York, Boston, Atlanta, etc.

Q8. Will a criminal record prevent me from getting a visa?

A. It depends on the severity and type.

Usually rejected:

  • DUI/DWI (especially recent)
  • Drug-related offenses
  • Violent crimes
  • Sex offenses

Usually accepted:

  • Minor traffic violations (speeding, parking tickets)
  • Very old non-violent misdemeanors (10+ years ago)

Q9. Can I reuse an FBI check from 8 months ago?

A. No. FBI background check must be issued within 6 months of when the employer applies for VIN.

An 8-month-old check cannot be used, so you need to get a new one.

Q10. Can my spouse and children come with me?

A. Yes, on F-3 dependent visas. However:

  • Separate visa applications required
  • Additional documents needed (marriage certificate, birth certificates - all apostilled)
  • They cannot work in Korea
  • Consider if single income is sufficient
